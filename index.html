About Philippe Starck

Philippe Starck is a famous French designer known for furniture, interiors and household products. His work often combines function, creativity and bold modern style.

He is important for GCSE DT because his products show how everyday objects can be both practical and visually striking.

Design Style

Modern

Clean shapes and simple forms.

Creative

Ordinary products look unusual and exciting.

Functional

Designed to be useful in everyday life.

Innovative

Experiments with materials and shape.

Famous Products

  • Juicy Salif – a famous lemon squeezer with a tripod form.
  • Louis Ghost Chair – a transparent modern chair.
  • Interior Design – hotels, restaurants and stylish spaces.
  • Household Products – furniture, bathroom items and kitchen products.
Philippe Starck’s work shows that products can be useful, artistic and memorable at the same time.

Colours and Materials

Metallic Silver

Creates a sleek, futuristic look.

Transparent Plastic

Gives products a light modern appearance.

Black and Neutral Tones

Feel elegant and sophisticated.

Glossy Finishes

Make products look eye-catching and premium.

Why He Is Important

Philippe Starck is important because he changed the way people think about everyday objects. He showed that products can be both useful and artistic. His work combines creativity, function and innovation.

Juicy Salif

Juicy Salif is one of Philippe Starck’s most famous products. It is a lemon squeezer created for Alessi and is known for its unusual tripod shape and futuristic appearance.

Designer

Philippe Starck

Manufacturer

Alessi

Material

Cast aluminium

Year

1990

Shape

Tall, slim and tripod-like.

Colour

Silver metallic finish.

Material Choice

Aluminium is strong and lightweight.

User Appeal

Eye-catching and memorable.

Advantages

  • Very stylish and modern appearance
  • Easy to recognise and memorable
  • Strong material
  • Creative and original design

Disadvantages

  • Not as practical as some other lemon squeezers
  • Can be harder to use
  • Sometimes valued more for looks than function
  • May be less comfortable for some users
